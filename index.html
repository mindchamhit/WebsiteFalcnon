About Our Nation

History of India

Indian history begins with the birth of the Indus Valley Civilization and the coming of the Aryans. These two phases are usually described as the pre-Vedic and Vedic age.The history of independent India or history of Republic of India began when the country became an independent sovereign state within the British Commonwealth on 15 August 1947.

Culture of India

India is one of the most religiously and ethnically diverse nations in the world, with some of the most deeply religious societies and cultures. Religion plays a central and definitive role in the lives of many of its people.

Heritage Of India

India is a land of varied cultures and traditions. People from numerous castes, religions, and creeds reside in our country. Each ethnic group in our country has its own tale of origin and its set of unique traditions and culture. They have all contributed to the making of Indian history and culture.

Our Culture

Knowledge And Stories

India's rich cultural tapestry is deeply rooted in its ancient knowledge systems and storytelling traditions. From the Vedas and Upanishads, which form the bedrock of Indian philosophy and spirituality, to the epics like the Mahabharata and Ramayana that weave intricate narratives of heroism, morality, and cosmic order, India's intellectual heritage is vast and profound.

Language

India's linguistic culture is a vibrant tapestry woven with rich diversity, reflecting its complex history and multicultural society. With 22 officially recognized languages and hundreds of dialects, each language represents unique traditions, literature, and oral histories. Hindi and English serve as link languages, facilitating communication across regions.

Arts

India's art culture is a rich tapestry woven from diverse traditions and histories. Spanning millennia, it encompasses classical dance forms like Bharatanatyam and Kathak, intricate music traditions such as Hindustani and Carnatic, and a plethora of visual arts, including Mughal miniature paintings and vibrant folk art like Madhubani.

Food and Drink

India's food and drink culture is a vibrant tapestry reflecting its diverse geography, rich history, and myriad cultural influences. Renowned for its variety and complexity, Indian cuisine encompasses an array of flavors, ingredients, and cooking techniques, varying significantly across regions. Staple foods like rice, wheat, and lentils are complemented by a plethora of spices, giving dishes their distinctive aromas and tastes.

Ancient Object

Ancient object culture refers to the practices and values surrounding the creation, use, and reverence of physical artifacts in ancient societies. These objects, ranging from tools and weapons to art and religious symbols, offer insights into the daily lives, technological advancements, and spiritual beliefs of ancient peoples.

Tradition And Rituals

India's rich cultural tapestry is woven with a myriad of traditional rituals and practices. Each region, religion, and community within the country brings its unique customs to the collective heritage. Rituals such as lighting oil lamps during Diwali, performing elaborate wedding ceremonies that vary by community, and celebrating harvest festivals like Pongal and Baisakhi are integral to Indian life.
